How We Operate

Who owns your cross-border care pathway?

Cross-border care requires more than coordination. It requires clearly defined ownership, escalation, and operating discipline before pathways are activated.

Pathway Design and Governance

Cross-border care pathways are designed with explicit ownership and governance structures defined upfront.


The focus is on ensuring that pathways are institutionally understood, clinically independent, and operationally defensible across participating entities.

Institutional Readiness

Institutions participating in cross-border care pathways must demonstrate readiness across clinical, operational, and governance dimensions.


This includes clarity on clinical independence, coordination responsibilities, and the ability to operate within defined frameworks once pathways are active.

Engagement Model

Engagements are advisory and governance-led, focused on the upstream design and operating discipline of cross-border care pathways.

We do not:

  • •market or solicit patients
  • •operate referral-fee, finder-fee, or incentive-based models
  • •influence clinical decision-making
  • •prioritise speed over safety
